🥛 Fermentation Project Site Plan 🧪

Jeimi Díaz Mariani

🏷️ Site Name

Name: FermentLab / Culture & Craft

Reasoning: I chose this name because it balances the scientific, biological nature of culturing wild microorganisms ("Ferment" / "Culture") with the hands-on, traditional domestic activity of preparing food at home ("Lab" / "Craft"). It sounds clean, authoritative, and welcoming for beginners.

🎯 Site Purpose

The purpose of this website is to provide an educational, user-friendly, and practical resource platform dedicated to the science and practice of homemade food fermentation, specifically focusing on Kefir and Yogurt production. The site will organize essential biological facts, step-by-step cultivation checklists, and troubleshooting guides to make the fermentation process highly accessible and error-free for beginners.

👥 Target Audience & Scenarios

🌱 Scenario 1: The Total Beginner

Question: I just bought my first milk kefir grains, but I don't know what kitchen tools are safe to use or how often I need to feed them. Where can I find a simple checklist?

Answer: The website will feature structural layout cards containing a "Basic Setup Checklist" highlighting material safety (like avoiding metal spoons) and a step-by-step guidance timeline for daily starter maintenance.

⚠️ Scenario 2: The Troubled Fermenter

Question: My homemade yogurt came out extremely watery and separated into whey. Is it ruined, or can I fix the temperature environment?

Answer: The platform will offer an intuitive troubleshooting guide section designed with clear typography hierarchy so users can rapidly diagnose production issues and understand the underlying biological factors of over-fermentation.

🎨 Color Scheme

The colors selected reflect cleanliness, water purity, and a professional laboratory environment, perfect for safe home fermentation tracking.

  • Primary Color: #0F2C59 (Deep Ocean Blue) - Used for major headers, primary titles, and structural borders.
  • Secondary Color: #FFFFFF (Pure White) - Represents milk, yogurt bases, and absolute sanitation. Used for content card backgrounds.
  • Accent Color: #00A8CC (Electric Cyan/Celeste) - Used selectively for subtitles, interactive highlights, and actionable buttons.
  • Background Color: #D0DCE5 (Muted Slate Blue) - A soft, medium-light grayish blue blend that provides enhanced visual depth and eye-comfort during long reading sessions.

🔤 Typography

Heading Font: "Montserrat", sans-serif. Used for all main headers to deliver a clean, crisp, and modern aesthetic.

Body Font: "Open Sans", sans-serif. Selected due to its outstanding legibility and soft contrast on responsive viewports during long structural reading sessions.
