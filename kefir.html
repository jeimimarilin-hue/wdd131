Kefir Preparation Guide

Kefir is a fermented milk drink that originated in the North Caucasus region. It is created by adding kefir grains to milk, which initiates a fermentation process that provides a unique tangy flavor and a wealth of probiotics.

Required Ingredients & Tools

  • 1 tablespoon of active kefir grains.
  • 2 cups of fresh whole milk.
  • A clean glass jar.
  • A plastic or wooden spoon (avoid metal if possible).

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Combine the kefir grains and milk in the glass jar.
  2. Cover the jar with a breathable cloth and secure it with a rubber band.
  3. Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 12 to 24 hours.
  4. Strain the liquid to separate the grains, and your kefir is ready to drink.

Safety Note: Always ensure your equipment is thoroughly cleaned to prevent the growth of unwanted bacteria during the fermentation process.
